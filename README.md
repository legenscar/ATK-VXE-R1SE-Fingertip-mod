# ATK-VXE-R1SE-Fingertip-mod
Fingertip Mouse mod for the ATK VXE R1SE+ PCB, featuring a flared pinky rest for a relaxed grip. Read through the whole README before starting.
  
## Things you will need: 
- a suitable screwdriver
- dot skates
- access to a 3D Printer or 3D Printing service
- Sanding paper (recommended but optional)
- Grip tape (recommended but optional)
- patience (recommended but optional)
- lighter (could come in handy)

## Assembly
### 1. Download and print all of the STL files.
I recommend rotating the main buttons 90 degrees if you are printing on a FDM to make sure they feel smooth. Dont forget the supports!

<img width="530" height="361" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f414c1e5-1c12-423f-ae84-0c888050708d" />

### 2. Disassemble the original mouse
Be careful not to rip the side PCB ribbons. Keep the screws and keep track of their mounting points. The printed mouse shell uses those exact screws with identical mounting points. You will also need to remove the screws for the main buttons.
  
### 3. Assemble the shell
First, screw the PCB onto the base. Dont forget to put the lense underneath first.

<img width="4096" height="3072" alt="Base with PCB" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/74687047-afa0-4ecb-9df2-0f963f2baf24" />

Next, lets take care of the side PCB. For that, mount the side PCB onto the support frame as shown in the picture and insert the side buttons. You may need to sand the "leg" if they dont fit, or glue them into place if they are too lose. If you experience excessive pretravel apply some tape. 

<img width="4096" height="3072" alt="Side PCB assembly" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bfc70bc7-d8d9-48f3-aff5-525c0ca90ea6" />

You will need to cut the little "tail" of the side PCB if you dont want it sticking out. I suggest you make that decision after you have assembled the whole mouse. 

Plug in the battery and stick it onto the sensor. Now you can mount the whole support assembly onto the base. First put the side with the buttons in, then the other side. Screw it into place from the bottom.

<img width="4096" height="3072" alt="support in" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c33afa24-a600-47aa-8a28-c50cd6c4350b" />

Last but not least, mount the main buttons and screw them in. Congratulations, youre done! If you experience excessive pretravel you can heat up the "beams" of the main buttons (only if you are using certain plastics like PLA that melt easily and do not give of (very) toxic fumes) and bend them a little. 

<img width="4032" height="3024" alt="Finished mouse" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/60a959a0-d391-4516-a68d-f6b0743866a8" />

## Notes
- I printed my mouse out of PLA using a Bambu Lab P1S with 0.2mm layer height and standard settings
- Due to the difference in printers and filaments I cannot gurantee that this model works with your setup. I believe in most cases some glue and sandpaper should do the job though.
- The finished mouse might not look 100% like the one in the pictures as I updated some things for better fit.
- This design will probably not work with any other R1 series mice due to the difference in sensors, PCBs (and lenses). Feel free to modify it for those.
